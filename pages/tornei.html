TOURNAMENTS

Australian Open

Grand Slam • 12-25 January 2026 • Melbourne, Australia

The first Grand Slam of the season, played on hard courts under the intense Australian summer heat. A fast-paced tournament that often sets the tone for the entire tennis year.

Indian Wells (BNP Paribas Open)

ATP Masters 1000 • 4-15 March 2026 • Indian Wells, USA

One of the most prestigious ATP Masters events, played in the California desert. Known for its large crowds, slow hard courts, and a stadium atmosphere often called the “fifth Grand Slam.”

Miami Open

ATP Masters 1000 • 18-29 March 2026 • Miami, USA

The second half of the famous “Sunshine Double.” Played on outdoor hard courts in a vibrant atmosphere, this event closes the major North American spring swing.

Monte-Carlo Masters

ATP Masters 1000 • 6-12 April 2026 • Monte Carlo, Monaco

The traditional start of the European clay-court season. Set above the Mediterranean, this historic tournament challenges players with slow clay and long tactical rallies.

Madrid Open

ATP Masters 1000 • 22 April-3 May 2026 • Madrid, Spain

A high-altitude clay tournament that produces faster conditions than most clay events. Madrid combines intense matches with a modern stadium atmosphere.

Italian Open (Internazionali d'Italia)

ATP Masters 1000 • 6-17 May 2026 • Rome, Italy

One of the most historic clay tournaments in tennis. Played in Rome•s Foro Italico, it serves as the final major preparation before Roland Garros.

French Open (Roland Garros)

Grand Slam • 24 May-7 June 2026 • Paris, France

The world•s premier clay-court Grand Slam. Famous for its slow surface, demanding rallies, and legendary matches played on the iconic courts of Roland Garros.

Wimbledon

Grand Slam • 29 June-12 July 2026 • London, United Kingdom

The oldest and most prestigious tennis tournament in the world. Played on grass courts at the All England Club, it represents tradition, elegance, and elite competition.

US Open

Grand Slam • 31 August-13 September 2026 • New York, USA

The final Grand Slam of the season, held in New York. Known for its energetic crowds, night matches, and the massive Arthur Ashe Stadium.

ATP Finals

ATP Finals • 15-22 November 2026 • Turin, Italy

The season-ending championship featuring the top eight players in the world. A high-level indoor event that crowns the final champion of the ATP season.
